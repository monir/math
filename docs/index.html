An open repository of mathematical research on hard, classical problems — ellipse perimeters, snow-crystal physics, the λ-ladder of log primitives, and the UNDERWOOD framework that ties them together. Every result is reproducible, every coefficient is written out, every proof is checked by Lean 4 where possible.

This site is the public landing page for the monir/math repository. The repo is laid out as one folder per mega-topic (ellipse/, snowcrystal/, …), each with its own paper, code, scripts, and Lean modules. New topics are added as their own subdirectories rather than sprawling sideways through the same tree.

Most readers want one of three things: (a) the shortest accurate ellipse-perimeter formula they can copy into a CAD tool; (b) the full proof of the world record with every coefficient and every bound; (c) the scripts that reproduce the numbers at 50–300 digits in seconds. The featured-record card below is for (a); the linked paper is for (b); the per-topic scripts/ folder is for (c).

The UNDERWOOD F4 formula in fully written-out form (every coefficient verified at 50-digit mpmath):

$$ P(a,b) = \tfrac{a+b}{2}\,\Bigl[ \sum_{k=0}^{8} c_k\,T_k(u) \;+\; \sum_{j=0}^{4} d_j\,\xi^{2+j}\,\ln(1/\xi) \Bigr] $$ $$ \text{with}\quad h = \bigl(\tfrac{a-b}{a+b}\bigr)^{2},\quad \xi = 1-h,\quad u = 2\xi - 1. $$

14 stored constants. Every $c_k$ and $d_j$ is listed exactly in the paper’s cookbook section (and in scripts/underwood/formula_renderer_dual_v1.py, so the paper can never drift from the running code).

logatoms & the $\lambda$-ladder

shared infrastructure

The logatom primitive $M^{j}_{\alpha}(\xi) = \xi^{\alpha}(\log 1/\xi)^{j}$ that lets UNDERWOOD work on the ellipse, $K(m)$, $Y_0$, $r^{2/3}$ corners, and Lamé functions all by changing the singular-mode list. Developed in standard real analysis (Chebyshev, Bernstein, Heisenberg ladder on Laguerre frames); Odrzywołek's recent EML grammar (arXiv:2603.21852) is a parallel encoding the logatoms also inhabit, but is not a substrate UNDERWOOD's proofs depend on.
